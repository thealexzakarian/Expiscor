The Immortal Jellyfish Project

Unlocking Nature's Secrets to Reverse Aging

Problem

Human aging and degenerative diseases continue to be one of the greatest biological and medical challenges. Cellular senescence, DNA damage, and limited regenerative capacity hinder our ability to reverse aging.

Project Overview

This project investigates the unique regenerative and life cycle-reversal abilities of Turritopsis dohrnii, the only known biologically immortal animal. Through genomic research, we aim to understand the mechanisms behind its transdifferentiation, DNA repair, and telomere maintenance.

Goals

  • Decode the genetic pathways responsible for cellular rejuvenation.
  • Identify key proteins and regulatory factors involved in transdifferentiation.
  • Explore applications of jellyfish genetics in anti-aging and regenerative medicine.

Vision

Solution Approach

  • Whole-genome sequencing and comparative analysis.
  • Functional studies of stem cell and DNA repair genes.
  • Application of bioinformatics and CRISPR-based gene analysis.
